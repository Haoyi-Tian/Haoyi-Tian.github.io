RC Airplane Design and Build Project I - WIP

Description:

The ultimate goal of this project was to build an airplane that could successfully fly. As this was my first plane, it also served as a great learning experience (especially from all the mistakes and troubles along the way).

This image is of the completed aircraft, which I was able to successfully pilot (briefly) under windy conditions after several attempts. There are a few potential causes for the diffuculties, in both the aircraft's build design and in my piloting abilities. These will be summarized below and used to improve the performance of the next aircraft.

Process:

The RC airplane was primarily constructed out of cardboard from a large Amazon box. Large rectangular panels were cut to be folded into airfoils for the wings. As shown in the photo, the cardboard was repeatedly creased near the leading edge to allow for a curvature in the upper surface of the wing. The lower surface, which is shorter than the upper, remained flat.

Next, strips of cardboard were cut and bent into thin supports (the long pieces resting on the not-yet-assembled wings in the photo) that were inserted into the wings to improve rigidity in the airfoil shapes. Hot glue was used to attach the supports at the thickest point of the airfoil. The surfaces of the wing were then glued together near the trailing edge. An extra flap of cardboard was left attached to each wing, to be used as the ailerons.

This image shows the completed wing structure with the fuselage and tail in progress. The two wing pieces were glued together with a dihedral angle for increased roll stability.
